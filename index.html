Participate | 2022.10 - 2023.10

This project is supported by Bytedance Inc. and focus on the micro-service migration problem with affinity, stemming from the cloud computing industry. With the fact that micro-services are invariably changing for satisfying users' demands, the deployment of micro-services has to be regularly adjusted, which is referred to micro-service migration. An optimal migration method should minimize the nubmer of activated physical machine and migration steps as well as maximize services' total internal affinity. This kind of problem requires such a large dataset and complex modeling that traditional methods are hard to calculate the most probable situation. We propose a cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learing(MARL) algorithm and improve its efficiency by fine-tuning a pre-trained ResNet model. We validate the proposed MARL on both synthetic datasets and real cloud traces of ByteDance,compared to two heuristic benchmark approaches: Migration Ant Colony Optimization and Migration Neighborhood Search. Finally we propose an evaluation mechanism called the Matching Score to explain the superior performance of MARL.

In this project, I was mainly responsible for the construction of heuristic algorithms and data mining. I have tried Bestfit, Firstfit, annealing algorithm and other benchmark algorithms. At the same time, I assisted my supervisor (Second Author) and his postgraduate student (First Author) to build the basic reward structure of multi-agent. Through this project, I have gained more understanding of operations research and reinforcement learning.
