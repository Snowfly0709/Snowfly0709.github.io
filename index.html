Bytedance Research Project "Data Center Micro-service Scheduling (Phase II)"

Participate | 2022.10 - 2023.10

This project is funded by Bytedance Inc. and focuses on the micro-service migration problem with affinity, stemming from the cloud computing industry. Because micro-services are invariably changing to satisfy users' demands, the deployment of micro-services has to be regularly adjusted, called micro-service migration. An optimal migration method should minimize the number of activated physical machines and migration steps as well as maximize services' total internal affinity. This kind of problem requires such a large dataset and complex modeling that traditional methods make it hard to calculate the most probable situation. We propose a cooperative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learing(MARL) algorithm and improve its efficiency by fine-tuning a pre-trained ResNet model. We validate the proposed MARL on both synthetic datasets and real cloud traces of ByteDance, compared to two heuristic benchmark approaches: Migration Ant Colony Optimization and Migration Neighborhood Search. Finally, we propose an evaluation mechanism called the Matching Score to explain the superior performance of MARL.

In this project, I was mainly responsible for the construction of heuristic algorithms and data mining. I have tried Bestfit, Firstfit, annealing algorithm and other benchmark algorithms. At the same time, I assisted my supervisor (Second Author) and his postgraduate student (First Author) to build the basic reward structure of multi-agent. Through this project, I have gained more understanding of operations research and reinforcement learning.

Course Project "Research on application of satellite navigation system in the field of deep space navigation"

Host | 2024.05 - 2024.08

This project is supported by Professor Yikang Yang who works at the Manned Lunar Landing Expert Group and focuses on the probability of using satellite navigation in deep space navigation. It's very difficult to navigate outside the solar system with existing navigation conditions, and the only example so far is Voyager 1. In the absence of a fixed reference system, this project studies asteroid avoidance and star navigation positioning, trying to provide a little inspiration for possible interstellar navigation in the future. We constructed a mathematical model named the spacecraft satellite ring and employed four-point positioning in space to ascertain whether the spacecraft's path coincided with the asteroid's trajectory, thereby determining whether the avoidance action should be implemented. Compared with traditional celestial navigation and ground-based fixed deep space navigation, we propose that positioning accuracy can be effectively enhanced through the utilization of the adjoint satellite and the validity of this theory is demonstrated by mathematical calculation.

In this project, I primarily proposed the navigation model and established the mathematical model throughout. I acquired more experience in independently completing a project, and my mathematical and modeling capabilities were also manifested. Finally, I received full scores(GPA 4.3/4.3) from my supervisor.
